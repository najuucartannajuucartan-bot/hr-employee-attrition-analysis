HR Employee Attrition Analysis

Project Overview

This project analyzes employee attrition data to understand why employees leave a company.

The analysis was completed using Excel and Power BI. The goal was to identify factors that may influence employee turnover and provide recommendations to improve employee retention.

Business Question

What factors contribute to employee attrition, and how can organizations improve employee retention?

Tools Used
Microsoft Excel
Power BI
Project Process

1. Data Preparation

The dataset was reviewed and cleaned in Excel.

Tasks completed:

Checked for duplicate records
Reviewed data quality
Created Pivot Tables
Prepared data for analysis

2. Data Analysis

Pivot Tables were used to explore employee attrition patterns.

The analysis focused on:

Department
Overtime
Job Satisfaction
Work-Life Balance
Monthly Income

3. Dashboard Development

An interactive Power BI dashboard was created to visualize employee attrition trends and key HR metrics.

Key Findings
Department Analysis

Research & Development had the highest employee attrition.

Overtime Analysis

Employees working overtime were more likely to leave the company.

Job Satisfaction Analysis

Lower job satisfaction was associated with higher attrition.

Work-Life Balance Analysis

Employees with poor work-life balance showed higher attrition rates.

Income Analysis

Employees who left the company had lower average monthly income than employees who stayed.

Recommendations:

Reduce excessive overtime
Improve employee satisfaction
Support better work-life balance
Review compensation policies
Focus retention efforts on high-attrition departments
